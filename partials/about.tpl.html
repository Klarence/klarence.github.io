About

Klarence OuYang is a user experience professional who has dabbled in the web for nearly a decade. He specializes in front-end development (emphasis on mobile/responsive web design), interaction design, information architecture, and usability.

Jack of all trades, and Master of One. I am a voracious learner when it comes to web development; my predominant passion, however, is intuitive design. “Form, Follows, Function” is my favorite design principle. While I do believe in creating beautiful aesthetic designs, I believe that every design element must have a purpose—it must contribute to the whole. When designing, I work methodically with an emphasis on creating pixel perfect work.

I enjoying assisting my colleagues in overcoming their technical and sometimes very perplexing challenges. Discovering how technical and design elements mesh and achieve streamlined improvements as technology advances intrigues me. I use all information and learning to hone the development of my professional and personal discipline.

Throughout my life I have enjoyed assisting family and friends with their technical challenges and I now enjoy assisting business colleagues and the business community address their technical and related business issues.I have assisted others in developing functional and effective websites allowing them to reach a larger audience with more clarity and well structured messages. In conjunction with my peers at SJSU we implemented a Customer Relationship Management System for a local charity which allowed the charity to manage their database of donors much more effectively.

I have worked with Project Managers at CISCO to coordinate the necessary changes (updates and code fixes) to the applications used by the company’s sales force.

I am currently involved with exploring Usability and its relationship to design. I am currently the User Experience (UX) lead in charge of coordinating an effective design for the Cisco's partner tools (Web Applications).
